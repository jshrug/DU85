Why Global 84 exists

A trip this complex deserves one home base.

International trips live across multiple WhatsApp threads, shared documents, and frantic group emails at 6:14 AM in a hotel lobby. Global 84 collapses all of that into a single, polished mobile experience, designed for the way our cohort actually moves through Singapore and Vietnam.

Stays close to the moment.

Built phone-first. Whether you're queuing at customs or settling into a company visit, the next thing you need is one tap from the home screen.

Holds the cohort together.

One feed, one chat, one calendar, shared by all 22 classmates and program staff. Side-channels finally optional, not mandatory.

Made for two cities at once.

Singapore and Vietnam each get their own rhythm: weather, currency, hawker stalls, hidden cafés, and a feed of cohort favorites that grows in real time.

Capabilities

A purpose-built toolkit, not a generic portal.

Every screen earns its place: it has to be useful in transit, on hotel Wi-Fi, in a meeting room, between sessions. No filler. Just the practical cohort experience, made to feel premium.

Mobile-first PWA
installed in 30 seconds.

No App Store. No download queue. Open the link in Safari or Chrome, tap "Add to Home Screen", and the cohort experience lives next to Maps and Messages.

  • Works on iPhone, Android, iPad. Same single codebase.
  • Offline-aware: cached announcements load even on a flight.

Discovery that feels
like cohort wisdom.

Curated places, not a TripAdvisor dump. Saved spots, recommended by cohort members, and a one-tap path from I should try this to let's do it tonight as a cohort event.

Real-time cohort chat.

One stream, one source of truth. Replaces the WhatsApp side-channels with a moderated cohort thread.

  • Live updates, threaded discussion per event
  • 1,000-char limit
  • Last 100 messages, oldest-first

Private team rooms.

For your group's project. Roster, meeting schedule, and team-only chat, invisible to other teams.

  • Team-only meeting calendar
  • Strictly private. Admins outside the team see nothing

Photo translator.

Snap a Vietnamese menu, a Singapore street sign, and get the original text + clean English in seconds. Powered by Claude.

  • JPG / PNG / WEBP up to 5 MB
  • Camera capture supported on mobile
  • Copy-to-clipboard on the translation

Practical travel utilities, never further than the side drawer.

The unglamorous tools that make a trip feel less stressful, kept tucked into the navigation drawer so the main interface stays focused.

  • Currency converter: bidirectional USD ⇄ SGD and USD ⇄ VND with live rates.
  • My Files: your private document vault for boarding passes, visas, and confirmation documents.
  • Media library: pre-departure videos and articles for Singapore and Vietnam, curated by the cohort.
  • Shared gallery: photo grid tagged by city, with likes and one-tap downloads.

Secure, cohort-only access.

Email-link sign-in restricted to @du.edu. Security rules enforce membership at every step. Your data never leaks to another cohort.

Across the trip

A companion that changes role across the journey.

Global 84 isn't a single screen. It's a tool that moves from anticipation, to coordination, to memory.

Phase I.

Before departure

Welcome announcements, destination videos, and pre-loaded Explore listings build momentum and shared anticipation across the cohort.

Phase II.

In Singapore

Live weather, hawker stall recs, real-time chat, RSVPs to company visits, and currency conversion. The daily loop.

Phase III.

In Vietnam

Photo translator earns its keep on every menu. Team rooms facilitate project work. The gallery fills with the trip's best moments.

Phase IV.

After the trip

The shared chat, gallery, and event archive become the cohort's living memory, long after we're back in Denver.

Built to hand forward

Cohort 84 will use it. Cohort 85 can inherit it.

Global 84 was scoped from day one as a reusable platform. The cities, brand color, currencies, splash imagery, admins, and access domain are all variables, not assumptions buried in code.

A future cohort lead can fork the repository, work through a pre-launch checklist, and have a rebranded version live for their trip in roughly a week, without rewriting a single feature.

React + Tailwind Firebase Auth · Firestore · Storage Vercel deploy Anthropic Claude

A handful of variables. The architecture stays.
